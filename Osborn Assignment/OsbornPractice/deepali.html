Press Release

(Richmond, Indiana) Osborn, the world’s largest and most complete solution provider of high-quality surface treatment and finishing products, is pleased to announce that it has appointed Brian Cassady as its new Chief Executive Officer. Cassady joins Osborn after serving as President and CEO of Municipal Parking Services, Inc., a software-based parking management and violation detection systems technology company. At the helm of Osborn, Cassady will leverage his extensive manufacturing and international experience to drive the company’s plans for continued growth.

March 4, 2021
Product News

(Burgwald/Wunsiedel). All Dronco product labels have received a new design. Tidy and fresh, with clear user information and internationally understandable pictograms. Instead of the Dronco logo, they now carry the Osborn trademark. There are no further changes - the products have the same high quality as before. After a phase in which the Osborn and Dronco companies appeared with both trademarks (e.g. in image ads in trade magazines or on selected cut-off, roughing and flap discs), the next step in the merger of the two brands has now been taken: The Dronco logo was removed from the communication and replaced by the Osborn trademark. This step was chosen in order to continue the communication under one brand umbrella with the common Osborn brand. This does not change anything for the customers. They will continue to receive products of the highest quality. The site in Wunsiedel, with one of the most modern production facilities for the manufacture of cut-off and grinding wheels, will remain and ensure that customers receive their products on time in the usual manner. Everything also remains as it is for the ordering processes and purchasing. All customers continue to reach their accustomed contact persons. The Wunsiedel site continues to operate under the name of Dronco GmbH, the Burgwald and Haan sites under Osborn GmbH. From March the first Dronco products will appear in the new design.

March 16, 2021
Company News

(Burgwald/Wunsiedel).Good things can always be made better. This is how Osborn develops products in terms of performance and quality. Osborn is now presenting a new product line under the title "Xtreme". It includes both cut-off wheels and brushes with significantly increased performance characteristics. In the long term, the "Xtreme" designation will also replace the "Evolution" quality level, which has so far designated the highest-quality products in the Osborn product portfolio. Xtreme AS 60 X INOX: Performance in a new dimension The new Osborn Xtreme range starts with the new AS 60 X INOX cut-off wheel. With a service life increased by at least 25%, it sets new standards in the premium segment. The increase in performance results from the use of a special abrasive grain in combination with an improved synthetic resin bond, which has been optimised with functional additives with regard to mechanical and thermal stability. Sales Manager Johann Michel is convinced that the new discs will be well received in the market: "The discs will cost slightly more than the predecessor AS 60 W, but deliver 25% more Power. This not only significantly reduces the disc costs per cut, but also set-up times". Confirmed performance The full power of the discs is available with the least amount of force and thus ensures fatigue-free working. Tests in the Osborn/Dronco test laboratory and tests by an independent testing and certification laboratory confirm the new cut-off wheel's outstanding performance when used on mild steel. In terms of tool life, the AS 60 X Xtreme clearly outperforms the products of well-known top competitors. The new product is also convincing when used on stainless steel V2A and ranks among the most powerful cut-off wheels. Availability Xtreme AS 60 X INOX The new 1.0 mm thin cut-off wheels are available in the dimensions 105 mm, 115 mm and 125 mm - either in a 25-piece shrink pack or per 10 pieces in a sealed metal can, which additionally keeps the premium cut-off wheels fresh until the packaging is opened for the first time.
